## What Exactly Is NAD+ and Why Does Your Body Need It?

NAD⁺ (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide) is a naturally occurring coenzyme found in virtually every cell of your body. While it doesn't receive the same attention as vitamins or hormones, it's one of the most important molecules involved in keeping your cells functioning properly.

**What We Know**

Scientists know that NAD⁺ is essential for life. Every day, your cells rely on it to:

- Convert nutrients into cellular energy (ATP) through healthy mitochondrial function.
- Support enzymes involved in DNA repair.
- Activate proteins called sirtuins, which help regulate cellular health, stress responses, and healthy aging.

Without adequate NAD⁺, these processes become less efficient. Simply put, your cells can't perform at their best without it.

Researchers also know that NAD⁺ levels naturally decline with age. While the exact rate varies between tissues and individuals, studies consistently demonstrate a meaningful reduction over the lifespan. This decline is one reason NAD⁺ has become an important focus in longevity and metabolic health research.

**What Researchers Are Studying**

Scientists are actively investigating whether restoring NAD⁺ levels may influence healthy aging, cognitive function, metabolic health, exercise recovery, addiction medicine, neurodegenerative disease, and age-related illness. The research is exciting and continues to evolve, but many of these potential applications are still being studied, and more high-quality human trials are needed before definitive conclusions can be made.

These observations are anecdotal rather than established scientific findings, and individual responses vary. They shouldn't be interpreted as proven clinical effects of NAD⁺. ## Why Do NAD+ Levels Decline With Age?

One of the most common questions we hear is, "If NAD⁺ is so important, why doesn't my body simply keep making enough of it?"

The answer is that aging changes the balance.

As we get older, our bodies begin producing less NAD⁺ while simultaneously using more of it. Researchers believe this shift is driven by several biological processes that occur naturally with age.

**What We Know**

Scientists have identified three primary reasons NAD⁺ levels decline over time.

1. **Your body breaks down more NAD⁺.** An enzyme called CD38 becomes more active with age, particularly in the presence of chronic, low-grade inflammation. CD38's job is to consume NAD⁺, meaning more activity results in faster depletion.
2. **Your body produces less NAD⁺.** Your cells rely on an enzyme called NAMPT to recycle and create new NAD⁺. As we age, NAMPT activity gradually declines, reducing the body's ability to replenish its own NAD⁺ stores.
3. **Your cells demand more NAD⁺.** Every day your DNA experiences wear and tear. Repair enzymes, known as PARPs, depend on NAD⁺ to help maintain DNA integrity. Over time, as more repair work is required, more NAD⁺ is consumed.

Taken together, these processes create a perfect storm: your body is making less NAD⁺, breaking down more of it, and using a greater share of what's left.

**What Researchers Are Studying**

Researchers continue to investigate how declining NAD⁺ levels influence healthy aging, metabolism, cognitive function, muscle performance, and age-related diseases. While NAD⁺ decline is well documented, scientists are still working to determine how restoring NAD⁺ levels may affect long-term health outcomes.

**What We Commonly See in Practice**

Many clients don't come to us because they think their NAD⁺ levels are low. They come because they feel the effects they associate with getting older.

They describe having less energy than they used to, feeling mentally foggy, taking longer to recover after exercise, or simply noticing they don't bounce back the way they once did.

These symptoms can have many different causes and should never be assumed to result from declining NAD⁺ alone. However, they're among the most common reasons people become interested in NAD⁺ IV therapy and longevity-focused wellness programs.

## Does a Blood Test Actually Reveal Your NAD+ Status?

One of the most common questions we hear is: "If my blood work is normal, doesn't that mean my NAD⁺ levels are normal too?"

**What We Know**

Not necessarily.

Most blood tests measure what's circulating in your bloodstream — not what's happening inside your cells, where NAD⁺ performs its most important functions. A normal blood result doesn't always reflect tissue-level NAD⁺ activity in organs such as the brain or muscles.

**What Researchers Are Studying**

Recent studies suggest blood NAD⁺ levels may remain relatively stable even as tissue NAD⁺ declines with age. Researchers are now using advanced imaging techniques to better understand NAD⁺ inside the brain and other organs, where many of its biological effects occur.

**What We See in Practice**

Many guests tell us they feel persistently fatigued or mentally foggy despite routine blood work appearing normal. Those symptoms can have many causes, but they highlight why longevity medicine increasingly looks beyond conventional laboratory testing when evaluating overall cellular health.

> **Citation Capsule:** A 2026 study published in Nature Metabolism followed 237 healthy adults and found that while whole-blood NAD⁺ levels remained relatively stable with age, NAD⁺ within muscle, brain, and fat tissue declined significantly. These findings suggest that blood measurements may not fully reflect tissue-level NAD⁺ status, highlighting why researchers are increasingly studying intracellular NAD⁺ rather than relying on blood alone. ([Nature Metabolism](https://www.nature.com/articles/s42255-026-01540-w), 2026)

## Why Choose IV Delivery Over NMN or NR Supplements?

Both approaches aim to support NAD⁺ levels, but they're designed for different purposes.

**What We Know**

NMN (nicotinamide mononucleotide) and NR (nicotinamide riboside) are oral precursors that your body converts into NAD⁺ through natural metabolic pathways. They're convenient, well suited for daily use, and continue to be studied as part of long-term wellness and healthy aging strategies.

IV NAD⁺ takes a different approach. By delivering NAD⁺ directly into the bloodstream, it bypasses the digestive system and provides a controlled method of administration under the supervision of a licensed healthcare professional.

**What Researchers Are Studying**

Researchers continue to compare different methods of supporting NAD⁺ levels, including oral precursors and intravenous therapy. While both have demonstrated biological activity, scientists are still determining how differences in delivery, tissue uptake, dosing, and treatment frequency influence clinical outcomes.

**What We See in Practice**

For guests visiting Los Cabos, the choice often comes down to timing.

Many clients already take NMN or NR as part of their daily wellness routine and choose IV NAD⁺ when they're looking for a more intensive, session-based experience — whether to support recovery after travel, prepare for a demanding week, or complement their broader longevity strategy.

Rather than viewing IV therapy and oral supplementation as competing approaches, we often see them as complementary tools that may serve different goals.

## Frequently Asked Questions About NAD+ IV Therapy in Cabo

### Does NAD+ IV therapy actually work, or is it just hype?

NAD⁺ is one of the most researched molecules in longevity medicine, but it's important to separate established science from emerging evidence.

The strongest human evidence currently supports improvements in cognitive performance. A 2021 clinical trial found participants receiving IV NAD⁺ demonstrated significant improvement in 6 of 8 cognitive performance tests compared with 2 of 8 in the saline control group. Researchers continue to study its broader effects on healthy aging, metabolism, recovery, and cellular health.

### How long does a NAD+ IV session take?

Plan for approximately 90 to 120 minutes.

Your infusion is started slowly and adjusted throughout the session based on your comfort and response. While it may be tempting to infuse more quickly, slower administration is generally better tolerated and helps minimize side effects.

### Are there any side effects?

Yes — and we believe it's important to discuss them openly.

Some guests experience temporary flushing, nausea, tingling, or mild chest tightness, particularly early in the infusion or if the rate is too fast. These effects typically resolve quickly by slowing the infusion and are generally well managed under the supervision of a licensed healthcare professional.

### How is IV NAD+ different from NMN or NR supplements?

NMN and NR are oral NAD⁺ precursors designed for convenient daily supplementation. IV NAD⁺ delivers the coenzyme directly into the bloodstream, making it an excellent option for people looking for a supervised, session-based wellness experience.
